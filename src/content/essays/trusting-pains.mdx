# Is trust a zero sum game?

Short answer, No.

Actually, the premise is wrong.

Mostly because what was in mind while trying to get to the question was another thing.

Aim: Looking for a word that explains that trust is not just a simple yes no, O 1 binary life choice.

It sure as hell sounds like a more gradient level thing. Something that exists in a continuous manner rather than discrete in nature.

## What is a zero sum game?

It is one of the things that can be explained in game theory as; one man's gain results in another's lose.

It's a game of winners and losers. And each man seeks to maximize their gains.

<Quote>
  A zero-sum game is a mathematical and economic concept where one person's gain
  is exactly offset by another's loss. In these scenarios, wealth or resources
  are finite, no new value is created. If you total the winnings and subtract
  the losses, the net result is always zero
</Quote>

## The Trust Gradient:

In physics, a "gradient" represents a smooth, continuous change in value from one point to another (like temperature changing across a room).

A trust gradient perfectly describes a relationship where trust is constantly shifting, rising, or falling based on proximity and actions.

## The Credence Continuum

In philosophy and formal epistemology, "Credence" is the technical term for a person's degree of belief in something.

Unlike standard belief (which is binary: you either believe it or you don't), credence is measured on a strict continuous scale from 0 (absolute certainty that something is false) to 1 (absolute certainty that it is true).

## Just do it: Obeying God even when it doesn't make sense.

<Notebook>
<Pause variant="moon" />

Some religious people make it sound easy and may make others feel terrible about themselves when they have trouble obeying and trusting in God.

You should realize that there is nothing wrong with that. People are different and it's totally fine. It's important that we do not put all the focus on us,but on Him.

He is able to help us obey His word and trust Him. Well... it's true indeed. It theoretically sounds simple and maybe just easy. It is a little bit more different practically.

You might be tempted to say that if God says then I'll do it. It is true but you need to trust Him. And to trust God,you must know something about Him that enables you to obey Him even though you might have wanted to do it differently or it just doesn't add up.

He says this,then let's do it. But there are times when obeying God doesn't make sense. This is seen in both the old and new testament. When God,as a Mighty God of Israel, would give instructions to His chosen leaders and when God,as Jesus here on earth, would instruct different people.

Let's do some case studies:

1. To Joshua

God had told the children of Israel that they would possess lands. Jericho was a strong city. Its walls were so thick that chariots of horses would in fact run on top of.

They were also very tall which made it very secure for the citizens of Jericho. God instructed Joshua to go round the city for 7 days and on the seventh day go round 7 times.

This didn't make sense to Joshua and if we had live broadcasts on that day,the Israelites would tell them how hilarious Joshua was by making them go round a city. It didn't make sense. Maybe some of them were angry at Joshua and maybe others may have wanted to protest. Anyway they just did it.

Then the best part,the shouting. On the seventh day,after going round the city for 7 days,man they were tired😂😂,then there came Joshua who told them to shout for God had given them victory.

Hold on Joshua,what victory. Maybe they wondered in their heads. "Well... let's just do,"they said within themselves.

They did shout and man,the strong walls came down. Hell yeah 😂😂. It was also profound that it is said that the walls came down and formed a ramp into the city.

The thing that kept them out of the city became a highway into God's promise. All this because of obedience to a command that didn't make sense. Maybe the strong Israelites would have wanted to plan an attack,but no. They obeyed. Ain't this great. They sure did feel great and confused. They were shouting one minute,and walls were down the other😂😂.

2. Peter and his friends.

He worked all night fishing. He caught nothing. He gave up,washed the nets in the morning. And early on came Jesus who asked for his boat.

Jesus taught the crowd from it. He afterwards told Peter to throw the net into the deep.This maybe what was going on in Peter's head:Hold on Jesus,fish are caught at night.

And... I'm a great fisherman,I know my jam. Have you ever fished before. Ain't you a carpenter's son,you don't know nothing.

But no. They caught so much fish,they called for backup. Their nets almost tore. If there were Guinness world records back then, we'd have Peter with one😂😂.

Jesus had a better plan. He transformed the great fisherman to actually be a fisher of men😂😂. That's obedience again when it didn't make sense.
